AI Outrage Test

An interactive satire that checks whether your AI condemnation applies elsewhere.

Not a policy debate.
Just a consistency check.

Pick your hill — environmental impact, intellectual laziness, job loss, artistic exploitation, corporate power, hallucination & bias — and see whether your principle holds up in everyday life.

If it does: congratulations.
If it doesn’t: simmer down or tighten up.
If you just don’t like AI: that’s allowed too.

What This Is

A tongue-in-cheek flowchart designed to explore whether objections to AI are:

Broadly principled

Selectively applied (e.g., performative rationalizing of just not wanting to use it because it's new, annoying, foisted-upon you, and you're not a "techie")

Or simply aesthetic preference

No moral scolding.
No political framing.
Just structured self-reflection with a glow effect.

What This Is Not

Not an argument that AI has no risks

Not a claim that all objections are equivalent

Not a defense of corporations

Not a dunk on artists

It’s satire. With logic.

Why

AI debates often get loud.
This tool lowers the temperature by shifting the question from:

“Is AI bad?”

to:

“Do I apply this principle consistently?”

That’s a more interesting question.

How to Use

Open the HTML file in your browser and choose your hill.

One ending appears. Always.
